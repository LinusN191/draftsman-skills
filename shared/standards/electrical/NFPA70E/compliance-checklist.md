# NFPA 70E:2024 — Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist to verify that work on or near energized electrical equipment complies with NFPA 70E Article 130. Each item corresponds to a specific clause and must be demonstrated before work begins.

## Hazard Assessment

- [ ] **Risk assessment completed per §130.5(B)**
  - Has a qualified person or engineer identified all arc-flash hazards at the work location?
  - Has likelihood been determined using Table 130.5(C)?
  - Is there documented evidence of the assessment process (sketch, calculation, or table-method record)?

- [ ] **Shock hazard boundaries established per §130.4(C)**
  - Have Limited Approach Boundary (LAB) distances been calculated or identified per Table 130.4(C)(a) or (b)?
  - Have Restricted Approach Boundary (RAB) distances been established?
  - Are workers aware of which boundary they are approaching?

- [ ] **Incident energy determined (if arc-flash hazard exists)**
  - Has incident energy been calculated using IEEE 1584 or NFPA 70E Annex D methods, OR
  - Has the equipment table method (§130.5(G)) been applied?
  - Is the incident energy documented and attached to the equipment label or work plan?

## Equipment and Labeling

- [ ] **Equipment arc-flash labels posted per §130.5(H)**
  - Is an arc-flash label visible at every point of operation (control panel, disconnect, breaker)?
  - Does the label display incident energy (cal/cm²) and required PPE category?
  - Are labels legible and not obscured?
  - Have labels been updated following any system reconfiguration or fault-level change?

- [ ] **Equipment boundaries and access points identified**
  - Are shock approach boundaries (LAB, RAB) clearly marked or communicated?
  - Have access points to energized equipment been documented?
  - Is it clear to workers where the energized work area begins and ends?

## PPE Selection and Use

- [ ] **PPE category selected per §130.5(G) and §130.7(C)(15)**
  - Has the incident energy result been matched to a PPE category using Table 130.7(C)(15)(c)?
  - OR has a task-based PPE category been selected from Table 130.7(C)(15)(a) or (b)?
  - Has the selected category been communicated to all workers?

- [ ] **Arc-rated clothing ensemble assembled per §130.7(C)(16)**
  - Are all garments arc-rated with ATPV ≥ assigned category requirement?
  - FR shirt: _____ ATPV (cal/cm²)
  - FR pants: _____ ATPV (cal/cm²)
  - Arc-rated hood (if category ≥ 2): _____ ATPV (cal/cm²)
  - Are hard hat, face shield, safety glasses, and hearing protection available?
  - Are leather gloves and closed-toe shoes provided?

- [ ] **PPE condition verified before use per §130.7(E)**
  - Has each garment been inspected for tears, burns, or contamination?
  - Are care labels and laundering instructions complied with?
  - Have any damaged garments been removed from service?

## Work Authorization

- [ ] **Energized Work Permit (EWP) on file if energized work is performed per §130.5(F)**
  - Has an EWP been issued by a qualified supervisor before energized work begins?
  - Does the EWP include:
    - Date, time, and duration of work
    - Equipment name and location
    - Justification for why equipment cannot be de-energized (§130.2 requirement)
    - Hazard analysis summary
    - Required PPE category
    - Rescue and emergency contact procedures
    - Signature of authorized supervisor
  - Is the EWP retained on file per §130.5(F)(1)?

- [ ] **Deenergization is not feasible, OR LOTO procedures are in place per §130.2(A)**
  - If work is on de-energized equipment, has lockout/tagout (LOTO) per §110.43 been completed?
  - Are all energy sources isolated, locked, and tagged?
  - Has verification per §110.44 confirmed de-energization?
  - If energized work is necessary, has the justification been documented in the EWP?

## Training and Competency

- [ ] **All workers are qualified persons per §110.26**
  - Do workers have documented training on:
    - Recognition of electrical hazards
    - Arc-flash and shock risks
    - NFPA 70E requirements and this facility's electrical safety program
    - Proper use and care of arc-rated PPE
    - First aid and CPR
  - Are training records current (within required intervals)?

- [ ] **Work environment and supervision**
  - Is a qualified supervisor or safety observer present during energized work?
  - Are secondary qualified persons (rescue personnel) designated and standing by?
  - Has the work scope been reviewed with all participants?

## Incident Preparedness

- [ ] **Rescue procedures in place per EWP requirements**
  - Are emergency contact numbers posted and known?
  - Is first-aid and CPR capability available on-site?
  - Have rescue procedures been communicated to all workers?
