## Authoring conventions used throughout this plan

Every KS1700/ JSON file MUST include these top-level fields (mirroring BS7671/ pattern):

```json
{
  "clause_ref": "KS 1700 2018:<section-slug>",
  "transcribed_at": "2026-05-18",
  "transcribed_by": "DraftsMan skills repo",
  "verification_status": "draft-from-bs7671-derivative-needs-source-verification",
  "_verification_note": "<derivation-vs-verification statement>",
  "_title": "<KS 1700:2018 ... title>",
  ...content...
}
```

Standard `_verification_note` template (subagent should adapt minor wording per file):

> "Drafted by structural derivation from BS 7671:2018+A2 (KS 1700:2018 adopts BS 7671 substantially per Annex E). Clause numbers and structural content match BS template; Kenya-specific deviations (where applicable) are sourced from KEBS technical bulletins and engineering practice in Kenya. Final verification against the published KS 1700:2018 PDF required before downstream skills rely on tight tolerances."

Standard authoring rule: for any KS clause that Annex E adopts verbatim, **copy the entire content of the BS7671/ sibling JSON file** (including all tabulated values like Zs_max, mV/A/m, current ratings) and ONLY change:
1. `clause_ref` → `"KS 1700 2018:<KS-clause-slug>"`
2. `_title` → KS-equivalent wording
3. `transcribed_at` → `"2026-05-18"`
4. `verification_status` → `"draft-from-bs7671-derivative-needs-source-verification"`
5. Add `_verification_note`
6. Cross-references to other BS files become cross-references to KS1700/ siblings
7. Add `_ks_adoption_pathway` field: `"KS 1700:2018 Annex E §<X> — adopts BS 7671:2018+A2 <equivalent-clause> verbatim"`

This preserves engineering correctness (values are identical) while making the layer first-class for KE jurisdiction.

For files with KS-specific deviation (e.g., reg411-rcd-requirements.json §411.3.3), the deviation is documented IN the file with `_ks_deviation_from_bs` field explaining what changes.

# KS 1700:2018 — Compliance Checklist

Engineer-facing checklist for KS 1700 compliance in Kenya installations. Adopts BS 7671 §6 inspection framework substantially via KS Annex E with KS-specific additions noted.

## §411 — Automatic Disconnection of Supply (ADS)

- [ ] Earthing system type identified (TN-S / TN-C-S / TT)
- [ ] Ze declared by KPLC OR measured at intake position
- [ ] Each circuit's Zs ≤ Zs_max per Table 41.2 / 41.3 (KS Annex E adopts BS Tables verbatim)
- [ ] Required disconnection time achieved per Table 41.1 (0.4s for final circuits ≤32A @ 230V; 0.2s @ 400V; 5s for distribution circuits)
- [ ] **§411.3.3 (KS-specific):** 30mA RCD additional protection installed on **all** socket circuits ≤32A regardless of Zs compliance

## §415 — Supplementary equipotential bonding

- [ ] Supplementary bonding installed in all locations specified by §415 (bathrooms, kitchens with metallic plumbing, swimming pool zones, agricultural)
- [ ] Bonding conductor CSA ≥ 4 mm² Cu

## §544 — Main bonding

- [ ] Main earthing conductor sized per §544.1.1: ≥ half the largest service conductor; ≥ 10 mm² Cu for protected route; ≥ 16 mm² Cu for unprotected route between intake and MET
- [ ] All extraneous-conductive-parts bonded: water service entry, gas service entry, structural steel, lift rails (where present)
- [ ] Bonding conductor CSA ≥ 10 mm² Cu for service entries (PME systems require this minimum)

## §722 — EV charging (KS-routed to IEC 60364-7-722)

- [ ] KS 1700 has no native §722. KS Annex E §VIII directs to IEC 60364-7-722:2018 for EV charging requirements.
- [ ] For EV installations in Kenya, apply IEC 60364-7-722 with KS 1700 §411 / §544 / §415 baseline.

## §521 — Installation methods

- [ ] Method selected appropriate for environment (Reference Method per Table 4A)
- [ ] Bunching / grouping factors applied per Tables 4B1-4C1 (KS adopts BS verbatim)
- [ ] Ambient temperature correction applied per KS Annex G climate zone (NOT BS 7671's 30°C baseline)

## §522 — IP ratings

- [ ] IP rating selected appropriate for environment
- [ ] **Kenya-specific:** Outdoor installations in coastal zones use IP65 minimum due to humidity

## §6 — Inspection and testing

- [ ] Initial inspection conducted before energisation
- [ ] Continuity tests, insulation resistance tests, polarity tests, earth fault loop impedance tests, RCD tests recorded
- [ ] Inspection certificate signed by EPRA-registered electrical contractor
- [ ] Periodic inspection scheduled per EPRA regime: ≤5 years commercial/industrial, ≤10 years domestic

## Equipment

- [ ] All consumer-side equipment carries KEBS certification mark
- [ ] Cables comply with KS specifications (cross-references KS ISO 11801 etc.)
- [ ] Protective devices (MCB / MCCB / RCBO / RCD) carry BS EN 60898 or IEC 60898 markings (both accepted by KS 1700 §531)

## Documentation

- [ ] Installation drawings + certificates kept on site
- [ ] KPLC Wayleave reference recorded for the connection
- [ ] Ze measurement record signed by KPLC technician

## See also

- BS7671/compliance-checklist.md — for adopted-verbatim BS clauses
- `ks-unique-deviations.json` — full enumeration of KS-specific deviations
- `annex-E-bs7671-adoption-table.json` — clause-by-clause BS↔KS adoption type

Per KEBS publishing policy, KS standards are reviewed on a 5-year cycle. KS 1700:2018 is therefore expected to be revised c. 2024 (or later if delayed). The next edition will likely incorporate:

# Kenya — KS 1700 Adoption Pathway

## Legal mandate

The Kenyan Standards Act (Cap 496) is the legal basis for the KEBS Mark and KS standards as mandatory product/installation requirements. Under this Act, KEBS publishes Kenyan Standards (KS) which become binding through reference in subsidiary legislation.

KS 1700:2018 (Code of Practice for Electrical Wiring of Buildings) is the primary electrical-installation reference in Kenya. It is referenced by:
- The Kenya Electricity Act for installation inspection requirements
- EPRA Regulations for grid-connected installation compliance
- The Building Code (administered by county governments and KENHA for outdoor lighting)

## KEBS publishing process

KEBS issues KS standards on a 5-year revision cycle. The standards committee for electrical installations includes:
- KEBS technical secretariat
- Institution of Engineers of Kenya (IEK) representatives
- KPLC engineering representatives
- Academic institutions (University of Nairobi Faculty of Engineering, Strathmore Energy Research Centre)
- KEPSA private sector representatives

For KS 1700, the committee chose to adopt BS 7671:2018 substantially via Annex E because:
1. BS 7671 is well-established, comprehensive, and continuously maintained by the IET
2. Adopting BS 7671 substantially reduces KS authoring burden
3. Kenya engineers have widespread training in BS-derived practice (legacy of UK education and the historical adoption of UK standards)
4. Kenya-specific deviations are captured in KS Annex E and the additional Annexes (F, G, H) without duplicating the full BS content

## EPRA enforcement

EPRA enforces electrical installation safety through:

1. **Initial verification:** Every new electrical installation must be inspected before energisation. Certificate signed by EPRA-registered electrical contractor.
2. **Periodic inspection:** Commercial/industrial installations inspected ≤5 years; domestic ≤10 years. Certificate filed with EPRA + retained on site.
3. **Connection approval:** KPLC will not energise installations without a current EPRA inspection certificate.
4. **Penalties:** Operating without certification can incur fines and disconnection; serious safety failures can trigger criminal prosecution under the Electricity Act.

## Relationship to international standards

KS 1700 references international standards as follows:

| Topic | Routing in KS 1700 |
|---|---|
| Earthing systems, ADS | KS §411 adopts BS 7671 Reg 411 via Annex E |
| Cable ampacity | KS Appendix 4 adopts BS 7671 Appendix 4 via Annex E |
| EV charging | KS §VIII routes to IEC 60364-7-722:2018 (no native KS §722 in 2018 edition) |
| Symbols | KS Annex A references IEC 60617 |
| Equipment certification | KS §531 accepts BS EN 60898 + IEC 60898 markings |
| Climate adjustments | KS Annex G provides Kenya-specific ambient temperature baselines (KS-unique) |

## Next revision

KS 1700 next revision is expected c. 2024 (5-year cycle). Anticipated changes:
- Incorporation of EV charging clause (native §722)
- Solar PV / battery storage clauses aligned with IEC 60364-7-712 / 7-826
- Smart meter / prosumer provisions
- Updated KEBS certification mark requirements
