## Notes

- All receptacles NEMA 5-15 or NEMA 5-20 duplex **tamper-resistant (TR)** per NEC 2023 Article 406.12 (mandatory for 15/20 A 125 V receptacles in dwelling units).
- Mounting heights AFF (above finished floor): general 450 mm (~18") per typical US residential convention (NEC has no mandated height for general receptacles); above worktop / counter 1100 mm (~43" — 6" above standard 36" countertop); outdoor / garage / basement / laundry 1100 mm (~43") for workbench / appliance access.
- **Kitchen** (NEC 210.52(B)): two small-appliance branches (C01, C02) required minimum; each branch is 20 A; AFCI per 210.12(B) + GFCI per 210.8(A)(6) → combination AFCI+GFCI dual-function breakers.
- **Bathroom** (NEC 210.8(A)(1) + 210.11(C)(3)): GFCI receptacle on a dedicated 20 A branch; tamper-resistant + GFCI receptacle device for redundancy. Mapped to schema `special_location: bathroom_zone_3` (closest IEC analogue).
- **Garage** (NEC 210.8(A)(2)): GFCI required for all 15/20 A 125 V receptacles. Three receptacles on C05; schema `special_location: null` with info-flag.
- **Outdoor** (NEC 210.8(A)(3) + 406.9(B)): GFCI + weather-resistant (WR) receptacles + extra-duty in-use covers; IP66 in schema is the closest IP analogue to UL 514D + NEMA 3R weather-resistant rating.
- **Basement** (NEC 210.8(A)(5)): GFCI required for all 15/20 A 125 V receptacles. Three receptacles on C07; schema `special_location: null` with info-flag.
- **Laundry** (NEC 210.11(C)(2) + 210.8(A)(10)): dedicated 20 A branch (no other outlets) + GFCI per 210.8(A)(10). One receptacle on C08; schema `special_location: null` with info-flag.
- **General receptacles** (NEC 210.12(A)): AFCI protection on the 15 A branch (C03) feeding living room, bedrooms, and hallway. No GFCI required outside NEC 210.8 scope.
- All branch breakers **10 kA AIC** per NEC 110.9 + UL 489, matching the MAIN-PANEL PSCC of 10 kA.
- All circuits carry `tool_call_pending_for_zs_verification: true`; ground-fault loop impedance Zs delegated to `calc.zs_loop_impedance` per WI3 (US equivalent: NEC 250.4(A)(5) + 110.10).
- Cable type **NM-B copper** (Type NM-B per NEC 2023 Article 334), with bare copper EGC; 12 AWG for 20 A branches (per 240.4(D)(5) small-conductor limit), 14 AWG for the 15 A branch (per 240.4(D)(3)). Schema encodes as `PVC_70` insulation (closest analogue to the 60 °C-terminated NM-B assembly).
- Final cable routing by `cable-containment` skill.
- Drawing per AIA CAD Layer Guidelines 2007 layer naming: `E-POWR-RECP` (receptacles), `E-POWR-CIRC` (branch circuit polylines), `E-POWR-PANL` (MAIN-PANEL schedule callout).
