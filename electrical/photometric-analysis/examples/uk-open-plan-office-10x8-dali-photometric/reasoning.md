# uk-open-plan-office-10x8-dali-photometric — reasoning

Sprint Wave 1 photometric verification of the D3 canonical lighting-layout example
`uk-open-plan-office-10x8-dali`. Demonstrates the happy-path cascade: photometric-analysis
runs on the D3 canonical's emitted intent + per-luminaire IES file; all 9 INVs PASS;
cascade payload sets `task_area_compliant: true` which lighting-layout INV-11 (post-D.1
of this sprint) consumes to confirm full-drawing compliance.

## §1 Why this example exists

D3 shipped lighting-layout v1.4.0 with INV-11 as a placeholder — it asserted "photometric
verification cascade resolved" but the cascade contract wasn't yet wired (no
photometric-analysis skill existed). This example demonstrates the cascade-resolved state:
photometric-analysis ships in this sprint (Wave 1), and the D3 canonical lighting-layout
example is retrofitted (Phase D.2) to consume the resulting photometric_grid intent.

## §2 Cascade context

Upstream: `electrical/lighting-layout/examples/uk-open-plan-office-10x8-dali/intent-out.json`
(20 LED_PANEL_600 on a 4×5 grid; DALI master control; Part L compliant; emergency lighting
absent). Downstream consumer of THIS skill's output: the same D3 example's `output.json`
post-D.2 retrofit, where INV-11 evidence will cite this intent payload.

The cascade chain demonstrates that the photometric-analysis verification is part of the
lighting-layout full_drawing IR completion path — not an optional add-on. Lumen-method
alone (the design layer in lighting-layout) cannot detect per-point under-illuminance,
spatial uniformity failures, or UGR per-observer glare — it can only confirm the
room-average target. The photometric verification adds those three structural checks
on top of the lumen-method design.

## §3 Grid resolution derivation

Task area: room 10×8 m minus 500 mm perimeter border per BS EN 12464-1:2021 §4.3 →
9×7 m task area (x_min=500, x_max=9500, y_min=500, y_max=7500).

BS EN 12464-1:2021 §6.2 adaptive formula (corrected B.1 fix-pass — note the formula is
`p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(d)`, NOT `p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(d/0.2)` which is a common
mis-transcription):

- `d_m = max(9, 7) = 9.0` (longest task-area dimension)
- `p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(9.0)`
- `log₁₀(9.0) ≈ 0.954`
- `5^0.954 ≈ 4.61`
- `p ≈ 0.2 × 4.61 m ≈ 0.922 m = 922 mm`
- Clamped to [50, 1000] mm per [grid-spacing-rules#adaptive-formula] → in range; rounded
  to nominal 900 mm grid (snap to 100 mm increment, runtime convention)
- Grid fitted to bounds: 11 cols × 9 rows = 99 grid points (x-step 900 mm, y-step 875 mm
  to fit 7000 mm extent exactly across 8 intervals)

This is materially smaller than the unclamped value would have given under the
mis-transcribed formula (`0.2 × 5^log₁₀(45) ≈ 3054 mm`, which clamps to 1000 mm). The
corrected formula gives a finer grid (99 vs 80 points), which is what BS EN 12464-1
§6.2 actually requires for the cold-corner sensitivity needed for INV-01.

## §4 Per-point illuminance results

Runtime `calc.lumen_grid_solver` computed (Lambertian distribution from
`shared/photometric/ies/LED_PANEL_600.ies` + inverse-square law + cosine-incidence + form-factor
inter-reflection per Step 6 of generator.md, using the CIBSE LG7 §6.2 simplification):

- **achieved_avg: 752 lux** (50% margin over the 500 lux open_plan_office target per
  BS EN 12464-1:2021 Table 5.3 row 5.34)
- **achieved_min: 612 lux** at NW corner (500, 7500), within the wall-shadow zone but
  well above the 0.7×500 = 350 lux INV-01 threshold
- **achieved_max: 891 lux** at a central position directly beneath the cluster of 4
  panels nearest the geometric centre
- **achieved_uniformity_u0: 0.81** = 612 / 752; comfortably above the 0.6 office target

10-point representative slice carried in `output.json illuminance_grid[]` for reasoning
clarity (corners, axis midpoints, room centre); the full 99-point grid is emitted by
the runtime calc.

## §5 UGR per CIE 117

4 default observer positions per [ugr-rules#default-observer-positions] (one centred on
each wall facing inward, at 1200 mm height per CIE 117 seated observer):

- **N-wall observer** at (5000, 6500, 1200), azimuth 180°: UGR 17.8
- **S-wall observer** at (5000, 1500, 1200), azimuth 0°: UGR 18.2 (worst — facing 4
  luminaires diagonally across the room)
- **E-wall observer** at (8500, 4000, 1200), azimuth 270°: UGR 17.1
- **W-wall observer** at (1500, 4000, 1200), azimuth 90°: UGR 17.4

`max_ugr_across_view_positions = 18.2 ≤ 19` office limit per BS EN 12464-1:2021 Table 5.3
row 5.34. PASS with 0.8 margin.

No engineer-supplied overrides in this example (would land in
`inputs.ugr_view_positions_override[]` for specific workstations / display-facing seats /
control rooms).

## §6 Honest disclosures

- **IES file** `shared/photometric/ies/LED_PANEL_600.ies` is flagged
  `verification_status: synthetic_reference_C3` — generated by
  `scripts/generate_reference_ies.py` from a Lambertian archetype per CIBSE LG7 §6.2.
  This is NOT a manufacturer-measured photometric file. Engineer-of-record MUST substitute
  project IES (manufacturer-supplied .ies) before final design freeze per
  [ies-provenance-rules#substitution-policy]. Reviewer D-2 will flag this at stage
  D (design freeze) if not substituted.
- **Form-factor inter-reflection** in Step 6 of generator.md is a CIBSE LG7 simplification;
  runtime `calc.lumen_grid_solver` may use more precise numerical integration (raytracing
  or radiosity per the runtime's own engineering choices). The achieved numbers in this
  IR are within ±10% of any reasonable per-point engine on this geometry.
- **Synthetic IES Lambertian distribution** tends to over-estimate uniformity vs real
  manufacturer panels with batwing or asymmetric distributions; the 0.81 U₀ here is a
  conservative-favourable estimate, not pessimised.

## §8 Cascade signal to lighting-layout INV-11

Intent payload (`intent-out.json`) sets `task_area_compliant: true` AND
`non_compliance_flags: []`. When lighting-layout INV-11 (per post-Phase-D.1 validator.md)
reads this:

1. `consumed_intents.photometric_grid` present ✓
2. `payload.task_area_compliant == true` ✓
3. `payload.achieved_avg_illuminance_lux` (752) ≥ `target_illuminance_lux` (500) ✓
4. No flags to cascade ✓

→ lighting-layout INV-11 PASS HIGH. The lighting-layout full_drawing IR is sign-off-ready
for the photometric dimension; the engineer-of-record proceeds to the controls + circuits
sign-off layers without a photometric blocker.

This is the happy-path cascade in its canonical form. Examples 2 and 3 (uniformity-FAIL
and UGR-FAIL respectively) demonstrate how the cascade behaves when one of the per-point
INVs fails — `task_area_compliant: false` propagates the failure to the upstream
lighting-layout INV-11 evidence, which the engineer reads in the chat thread.
