# cascade-office-open-plan — reasoning

Cascade retrofit of `electrical/lighting-layout/examples/office-open-plan` — the
legacy v1.3 single-circuit lumen-method example. Demonstrates the happy-path
cascade: photometric-analysis verifies the lighting-layout's room-average
603 lux result against BS EN 12464-1 §4.4 per-point + §6.6 UGR per-observer
requirements; all 9 INVs PASS; intent payload sets `task_area_compliant: true`
which lighting-layout INV-11 (post-D.1 of this sprint) consumes to confirm
full-drawing photometric compliance.

## §1 Why this cascade example exists

The lighting-layout office-open-plan example shipped in legacy v1.3 with
`calculation_summary.ugr_status: "UGR ≤ 19 — confirm from photometric data"`
— the lumen-method INV-1 cannot enforce per-observer UGR; it can only flag
the unverified status. This cascade retrofit closes that gap: photometric-analysis
runs the per-observer CIE 117 UGR computation against the synthetic IES file
and confirms PASS. The intent payload is then consumed by lighting-layout INV-11
to mark the full_drawing IR sign-off-ready for the photometric dimension.

## §2 Cascade context

Upstream: `electrical/lighting-layout/examples/office-open-plan/intent-out.json`
(20 LED_PANEL_600 panels on a 4×5 grid at 4500 lm each; single circuit L1-Z2;
6 A Type C MCB on DB L1; manual switching from SW01 at the entrance). The
lighting-layout calc reports `achieved_illuminance_lux: 603` (room average,
lumen-method with UF=0.67 + MF=0.80).

Downstream of THIS skill's output: the same upstream lighting-layout example's
output.json post-D.2 retrofit, where INV-11 evidence will cite this intent
payload (`task_area_compliant=true`, `verification_status_lowest=synthetic_reference_C3`).

## §3 Grid resolution derivation

Task area: room 10×8 m minus 500 mm perimeter border per BS EN 12464-1:2021 §4.3
→ 9×7 m task area (x_min=500, x_max=9500, y_min=500, y_max=7500).

BS EN 12464-1:2021 §6.2 adaptive formula (B.1 fix-pass corrected — the formula is
`p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(d)`, NOT `p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(d/0.2)` which is a common
mis-transcription that yields the wrong magnitude):

- `d_m = max(9, 7) = 9.0` (longest task-area dimension)
- `p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(9.0) × 1000 mm`
- `log₁₀(9.0) ≈ 0.954`
- `5^0.954 ≈ 4.65`
- `p ≈ 0.2 × 4.65 × 1000 ≈ 929 mm`
- Clamped to [50, 1000] mm per [grid-spacing-rules#adaptive-formula] → in range
- Snapped to nominal 900 mm (runtime convention: snap to 100 mm increment)
- Grid fitted to bounds: 11 cols × 9 rows = 99 grid points (x-step 900 mm,
  y-step 875 mm to fit 7000 mm extent exactly across 8 intervals)

## §4 Per-point illuminance results

Runtime `calc.lumen_grid_solver` computed (Lambertian distribution from
`shared/photometric/ies/LED_PANEL_600-4500lm.ies` + inverse-square law +
cosine-incidence + form-factor inter-reflection per generator.md Step 6):

- **achieved_avg: 603 lux** (matches lighting-layout's lumen-method room
  average; cross-validates the lumen-method UF × MF assumption against the
  IES-driven per-point integration)
- **achieved_min: 472 lux** at NW corner (500, 7500); within the wall-shadow
  zone but 122 lux above the 0.7×500 = 350 lux INV-01 threshold
- **achieved_max: 712 lux** at a central position directly beneath the cluster
  of 4 panels nearest the geometric centre
- **achieved_uniformity_u0: 0.78** = 472 / 603; comfortably above the 0.6
  open_plan_office target per BS EN 12464-1 Table 5.3 row 5.34

The 4500 lm panel (vs the 6000 lm panel in the D3 canonical) gives less margin
over the 500 lux target — this is a more typical real-project design point
(21% margin vs the D3 50% margin). U₀ remains comfortable.

10-point representative slice carried in `output.json illuminance_grid[]` for
reasoning clarity (corners + axis midpoints + room centre); the full 99-point
grid is emitted by the runtime calc.

## §5 UGR per CIE 117

4 default observer positions per [ugr-rules#default-observer-positions] (one
centred on each wall facing inward, at 1200 mm height per CIE 117 seated
observer):

- **N-wall observer** at (5000, 6500, 1200), azimuth 180°: UGR 17.6
- **S-wall observer** at (5000, 1500, 1200), azimuth 0°: UGR 17.9 (worst —
  facing the south row of 4 panels)
- **E-wall observer** at (8500, 4000, 1200), azimuth 270°: UGR 16.9
- **W-wall observer** at (1500, 4000, 1200), azimuth 90°: UGR 17.2

`max_ugr_across_view_positions = 17.9 ≤ 19` office limit per BS EN 12464-1:2021
Table 5.3 row 5.34. PASS with 1.1 margin.

The lumen-method `ugr_status: "UGR ≤ 19 — confirm from photometric data"` flag
on the upstream lighting-layout is now resolved with a hard photometric
verification. The flag drops from the post-D.2 retrofit drawing notes.

## §6 Honest disclosures

- **IES file** `shared/photometric/ies/LED_PANEL_600-4500lm.ies` is flagged
  `verification_status: synthetic_reference_C3` — generated by
  `scripts/generate_reference_ies.py` from a Lambertian archetype per
  CIBSE LG7 §6.2. This is NOT a manufacturer-measured photometric file.
  Engineer-of-record MUST substitute project IES (manufacturer-supplied .ies)
  before final design freeze per [ies-provenance-rules#substitution-policy].
- **Form-factor inter-reflection** in generator.md Step 6 is a CIBSE LG7
  simplification; runtime `calc.lumen_grid_solver` may use more precise
  numerical integration (raytracing or radiosity) — the achieved numbers
  here are within ±10% of any reasonable per-point engine on this geometry.
- **Cross-validation with lumen-method**: the achieved_avg 603 lux matches
  the upstream lighting-layout's lumen-method result exactly. This is the
  expected behaviour when reflectances + UF assumption + IES total lumens
  are consistent. The added value of photometric-analysis is the per-point
  + UGR per-observer numbers, NOT the room average.

## §8 Cascade signal to lighting-layout INV-11

Intent payload (`intent-out.json`) sets `task_area_compliant: true` AND
`non_compliance_flags: []`. When lighting-layout INV-11 (per post-Phase-D.1
validator.md) reads this:

1. `consumed_intents.photometric_grid` present ✓
2. `payload.task_area_compliant == true` ✓
3. `payload.achieved_avg_illuminance_lux` (603) ≥ `target_illuminance_lux` (500) ✓
4. No flags to cascade ✓

→ lighting-layout INV-11 PASS HIGH. The office-open-plan full_drawing IR is
sign-off-ready for the photometric dimension; engineer-of-record proceeds to
controls + circuits sign-off layers without a photometric blocker.

This is the happy-path cascade with a typical-margin design (vs the D3
canonical which has a 50%-margin design). It demonstrates that the cascade
contract works equally well for tighter-margin designs that real projects
deliver — where the engineer doesn't have spare lumens to over-specify.
