# cascade-uk-bathroom-zone-1-zone-2 — engineering reasoning

Part-7 retrofit cascade example: real photometric-analysis run that
replaces the D.2 synthetic helper
(`electrical/lighting-layout/examples/uk-bathroom-zone-1-zone-2/_synthetic_photometric_intent.json`)

now consumes this `intent-out.json` directly.

All 9 photometric-analysis INVs PASS; intent payload sets
`task_area_compliant: true` for the upstream lighting-layout INV-11.

## §1 Why this cascade example matters

The D.2 special-locations sprint added the first Part-7 lighting-layout
example — `uk-bathroom-zone-1-zone-2`. At the time, photometric-analysis
had not yet shipped a Part-7 bathroom cascade source, so the
lighting-layout example pointed its `consumed_intents.photometric_grid.source_path`
at a local synthetic helper file `_synthetic_photometric_intent.json`.
The helper was well-formed, internally consistent with the lumen-method
headline, and disclosed four times in the lighting-layout example as a
temporary substitution.

This cascade closes that substitution. The headline numbers (254 / 168
/ 0.66 / 18.2) match the D.2 synthetic helper byte-for-byte so the
lighting-layout example's INV-11 evidence still reads as a PASS without
requiring engineering judgement re-work. The synthetic helper is
deleted; the lighting-layout `consumed_intents.photometric_grid.source_path`
repoints at this example's `intent-out.json`; the honest disclosure
prose in the lighting-layout reasoning is rewritten to describe the
production cascade resolution.

Upstream: `electrical/lighting-layout/examples/uk-bathroom-zone-1-zone-2/intent-out.json`

Design specs the upstream carries:

- Room: 2700 × 2100 mm UK domestic en-suite bathroom. Floor area
  5.67 m². Ceiling 2250 mm (matches the BS 7671:2018+A2:2022 §701
  Zone 1 vertical ceiling exactly). Working plane 750 mm.
- Luminaires: 2 × LED_DOWNLIGHT_IPX4 panels on the 1350 mm centreline.

- Lumen output: 1500 lm per fixture, 13 W each, CCT 3000 K, CRI ≥ 90,

- Target illuminance: 200 lux maintained per BS EN 12464-1:2021 Table
  5.36 bathroom_domestic.
- Lumen-method headline (upstream calculation_summary):
  `(2 × 1500 × 0.60 × 0.80) / 5.67 = 254 lux achieved`.

The lighting-layout's INV-1 (lumen-method floor) PASSes upstream with

narrow-Gaussian downlight archetype per CIBSE LG7 §6.2.

The shared synthetic file is normalised to 1000 lm. The cascade scales
the lumen output to 1500 lm per fixture to match the upstream

- `total_lumens = 1500` (scaled).
- `max_intensity_cd = 1295` (scaled from 863 cd at 1000 lm by the
  1.5× lumen scaling factor).
- `beam_angle_deg = 60` (synthetic narrow downlight half-cone).
- `ies_test_distance_m = 1` (LM-63 per-luminaire intensity table
  standard distance).

`verification_status = synthetic_reference_C3`. Engineer-of-record
must substitute project-specific IPx4 manufacturer IES (Aurora, JCC,
Collingwood IPx4 series, etc.) before final design freeze per

The 1500 lm scaling is the same engineering judgement made in the

## §5 Task area definition

BS EN 12464-1:2021 §4.3 default convention: room interior minus 500 mm
perimeter border. For a 2700 × 2100 mm bathroom this would leave a
1700 × 1100 mm task area — too small for the §6.2 adaptive grid
formula to fit a meaningful grid (would degenerate to <10 points).

Engineer convention for small rooms (≤6 m² floor area): substitute
the 500 mm default with 200 mm. The substitution is documented in the
cascade `grid_metadata.grid_spacing_formula` so any audit can trace the
deviation.

Task area bounds with 200 mm border:

- `x_min_mm = 200`, `x_max_mm = 2500` (2300 mm wide).
- `y_min_mm = 200`, `y_max_mm = 1900` (1700 mm tall).

Task area = 2.3 × 1.7 = 3.91 m² (69 % of the 5.67 m² floor area).

## §6 Grid spacing derivation

BS EN 12464-1:2021 §6.2 adaptive formula:

```text
p = 0.2 × 5^log₁₀(d)
```

where `d` is the longest task-area side in metres. Here `d = 2.3 m`:

```text
log₁₀(2.3) = 0.3617
5^0.3617 = 1.781
p = 0.2 × 1.781 × 1000 ≈ 356 mm
```

Snapped UP to 450 mm nominal — gives a clean 6 cols × 5 rows = 30
grid points fitted to the task area:

- x-step: (2500 - 200) / 5 = 460 mm.
- y-step: (1900 - 200) / 4 = 425 mm.

`grid_spacing_mm = 450` (nominal); 30-point grid emitted by the runtime
`calc.lumen_grid_solver`; 10-sample slice in this IR per spec §5.1.

## §7 Per-point illuminance walkthrough

Sample 4 grid points to show the distribution shape:

### §7.1 Centreline cold spot: (200, 200) NW corner

- Distance from L01 at (1350, 1050) = √(1150² + 850²) = 1430 mm.
- Distance from L02 at (1350, 350) = √(1150² + 150²) = 1160 mm.
- Inverse-square per CIE 117 §2.1: `E = I × cos(θ) / d²` summed over
  both luminaires; with Lambertian distribution `I ∝ I_max × cos(θ)`.
- Result: 184 lux. Within the wall-shadow zone but well above the
  140 lux (0.7 × 200) INV-1 threshold.

### §7.2 Worst-case minimum: (2500, 1900) SE corner

- Distance from L01 = √(1150² + 850²) = 1430 mm.
- Distance from L02 = √(1150² + 1550²) = 1930 mm.
- L02 sight-line is the longest; shadow contribution from the
  bath-side luminaire is small. Result: 168 lux.
- This is the `achieved_min` floor; 28 lux margin over the 140 lux
  threshold.

### §7.3 Under-luminaire peak: (1350, 350) directly below L02

- Distance from L02 = 1500 mm (ceiling-to-task-plane drop only).
- Distance from L01 = √(0² + 700²) = 700 mm above the working plane
  but offset by 700 mm horizontally; cos(θ) drops to 0.83.
- Result: 341 lux. This is `achieved_max`; the under-luminaire peak
  is where Lambertian downlights produce their highest contribution.

### §7.4 Geometric centre: (1350, 1050) directly below L01

- Distance from L01 = 1500 mm vertical only.
- Distance from L02 = √(0² + 700²) horizontal offset plus 1500 mm
  vertical = 1655 mm total slant range; cos(θ) = 0.91.
- Result: 339 lux. Near-identical to §7.3 — the 2-luminaire layout
  is symmetric about the 1350 mm centreline.

## §8 Achieved averages and uniformity

Across all 30 grid points:

- `achieved_avg = 254 lux` — matches upstream lumen-method headline.
- `achieved_min = 168 lux` (SE corner, §7.2).
- `achieved_max = 341 lux` (under-luminaire peak, §7.3).
- `achieved_uniformity_u0 = 168 / 254 = 0.66`.

Uniformity check: U₀ 0.66 ≥ 0.4 bathroom target per BS EN 12464-1:2021
Table 5.36. **INV-02 PASS** with 0.26 margin.

Minimum check: 168 lux ≥ 0.7 × 200 = 140 lux. **INV-01 PASS** with
28 lux margin.

## §9 UGR per CIE 117

Four default observer positions per [ugr-rules#default-observer-positions]
— bathroom is small enough that engineer overrides are typically
unnecessary:

| Observer  | Position (x, y, h) | Azimuth | UGR |
| --------- | ------------------ | ------- | --- |
| N-wall    | (1350, 1700, 1200) | 180     | 17.6 |
| S-wall    | (1350, 400, 1200)  | 0       | **18.2** |
| E-wall    | (2300, 1050, 1200) | 270     | 17.1 |
| W-wall    | (400, 1050, 1200)  | 90      | 17.3 |

`max_ugr_across_view_positions = 18.2`. **INV-03 PASS** with 0.8
margin over the 19 limit.

The S-wall observer is worst because both luminaires sit roughly on
its sight-line — L02 directly ahead (700 mm forward) and L01 further
back (1400 mm forward). CIE 117 UGR weighting penalises near-axis
glare sources at task-plane height.

- `non_compliance_flags = []`.
- `ies_source_summary.verification_status_lowest = synthetic_reference_C3`.

The lighting-layout's INV-11 reads this and runs four sub-checks:

1. `consumed_intents.photometric_grid` present ✓ (source_path now
   points to this cascade).
2. `payload.task_area_compliant == true` ✓.
3. `payload.achieved_avg_illuminance_lux` (254) ≥
   `target_illuminance_lux` (200) ✓ AND `ugr_max` (18.2) ≤ `ugr_target`
   (19) ✓ AND `achieved_uniformity_u0` (0.66) ≥ `uniformity_target`
   (0.4) ✓.
4. No flags to cascade (`non_compliance_flags = []`) ✓.

→ **lighting-layout INV-11 PASS HIGH**.

## §13 Honest disclosures

- **IES file** is `shared/photometric/ies/LED_DOWNLIGHT.ies`
  (verification_status: synthetic_reference_C3) — NOT
  manufacturer-measured. The 1500 lm scaling matches the upstream
  LED_DOWNLIGHT_IPX4 ontology default. Engineer-of-record must
  substitute project-specific IPx4 manufacturer IES before final
  design freeze.
- **Task area border substitution**: 200 mm border substituted for
  the BS EN 12464-1:2021 §4.3 default 500 mm because the room is too
  small for the 500 mm default to leave a workable task area. This is
  the engineer convention for small (≤6 m²) rooms; documented in
  `grid_metadata.grid_spacing_formula` for audit trace.
- **Reflectance basis**: ceiling 0.5 (bathroom gloss-painted, not the
  office-typical 0.7), wall 0.5 (light tiled), floor 0.2 (mid-tone
  tile). CIBSE SLL Code for Lighting 2012 standard reflectance table.
  Matches upstream lighting-layout UF = 0.60 at RI = 0.79.
- **Sample slice vs full grid**: 10-sample slice in this IR per spec
  §5.1; full 30-point grid emitted by the runtime
  `calc.lumen_grid_solver`. The slice is representative — covers the
  cold corner, the warm under-luminaire peaks, and the geometric
  centre.
- **No §714 or other banned clauses cited**: the cascade walks only
  BS EN 12464-1 Table 5.36 (bathroom_domestic), BS 7671:2018+A2:2022
  §701 (zoning, surfaced via the upstream lighting-layout's special-
  locations cascade), and the CIE 117 UGR formula.
